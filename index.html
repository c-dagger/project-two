About Me

Student

I am a Graduate student in the Information Technology and Management Program at Illinois Institute of Technology. Previously graduated with a Bachelor of Business Management and Marketing at University of Wisconsin - Parkside.

Family

Yes I am a Dugger. No I am not related to the family on the TLC show "19 Kids and Counting". Although, I am the oldest of five and my youngest sibling is 18 years my junior. We're not your average family, but we definitely do not disappoint!

I grew as the pack leader in my family with each sibling after me 4 to 5 years apart from each other. We were told by family and friends growing up that it looked like we were coming out of a clown car when we arrived anywhere. I guess our teachers probably felt the same as we are all going to enter high school the year after the previous one of us graduated. The order would go: myself (graduated 2011), Zach (2015), Jacob (2019), Mackenzie (2024), and Ryleigh (2029).

We grew up all really close whether it was playing sports, helping each other with school, playing pranks at home, or growing up at our campground. Ironically, we actually have a golf cart train that is absolutely necessary for us to get around our campground together. We call it the "Chugga Dugga Train" as it sounds like a train. We still hold this tradition close to our hearts as we get older and try to make it up to our camper as often as possible. My mom and dad still go every weekend and have successfully convinced several other friends and family members to get their own campsites to keep them company. Like I mentioned before, never a dull moment. My parents married young and recently celebrated their 27th wedding anniversary. I am lucky to be so close to them and am also now at that stage in life where my mom refers to my dad as "Phil" instead of "Your Dad" and I am more of a best friend, but I wouldn't change our relationship for the world.

Fun

Camping

My family and I have gone camping since I was a little girl. Over the years, we have evolved from borrowed tents, to our own tents, to a pop-up trailer, to a stationary trailer, to what we have now - an RV trailer. My parents are avid campers so I have always cherished the idea of family time outdoors, with limited cell phone connection. There is no better feeling than listening to 80s rock, making smores in your favorite hoodie, going on a late night golf cart ride by the beach, and cracking jokes with your family by the campfire.

Travelling

Travelling is one of the greatest thrills in my life. I try to go to at least one destination vacation a year. I love travelling because it is a reality check that bursts our every day bubble. It is a notion that there is so much more to life than just our immediate surroundings, our every day stressors. It is hope and courage that you can always change your life and move in a new direction, literally. Travelling teaches us to be humble and grateful for what we have and to remind us that life is too short to stress on the little things.

Record Collecting

Music has always kept me grounded through every stage in life. I listen to music, dance to music, or even break out into song multiple times a day on a regular basis. It's quite entertaining. Recently, I picked up a 7 in 1 Victrola Turntable to play records on. I love all genres of music but majority of my favorite music can be found on classic records. Something about the tangibility of having that record in my hands, being able to change it manually, putting that needle on the vinyl so carefully separates the feeling so far from playing it somewhere digitally by clicking a button.
